In 2019, Warwick Congress bravely took on the growing and rapidly evolving Fourth Industrial Revolution, leading with the question 'where is humanity headed?' The 2-day conference focused on the economic, social and environmental consequences of the Fourth Industrial Revolution on businesses, governments and society as a whole.

Notable speakers included Abhijit Akerkar, Marina Theodosiou, Brigette Hyacinth and Ian West.

In 2018, Warwick Congress explored innovation and the social media revolution. Delving deep into the topic of Global Trade vs Embargoes, the conference addressed a new virtual era where goods and services are delivered to doorsteps everyday. Warwick Congress sought to provide a dynamic insight into whether the populist movements of the last few years are here to stay, or whether globalisation will pervade our future. With protectionist policy promised by the US administration and the uncertainties of Brexit looming over Europe, we asked: 'what is the fate of global free trade?'

Notable speakers included: Maria Carvalho, Michael Lewis, Dr. Katrin Tinn, Dr. Clair Gammage and Professor Gabriel Felbermayr.

2017 marked the first annual conference of Warwick Congress, welcoming 500 delegates and 12 speakers to address 3 topics through different perspectives. Under the leadership of over 45 students, Warwick Congress aimed to explore issues in finance, law, politics and economics, to build an awareness of how these interconnected disciplines shape the discourse of our world. The themed categories of discussion were the environment, technology and Europe.

Notable speakers included: Abhinay Muthoo, Diana Wallis, Tony Cocker and Miriam Maes.
