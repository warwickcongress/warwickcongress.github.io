Conferences

Our annual Warwick Congress conference centres around Finance, Law, Politics and Economics

2023

In 2023, Warwick Congress introduced the 'Roaring 20s'. The American epithet for the 1920s, it refers to a decade of prosperity, innovation, and cultural dynamism. Immediately following the First World War, this decade saw Western civilisation thrive. The Roaring 20s were a brief era defined by flourishing consumer demand, rapid industrial growth, and artistic revolution, something we aimed to include.

Notable speakers include: Nitin Sharma returning from 2020, Dana Denis-Smith, Mike Bagguley and firm representatives from Clifford Chance.

2021

In 2021, Warwick Congress continued 2020's mission of encouraging varied and diverse academic discussion. We remotely delivered training sessions and personalised CV workshops to make careers in Law, Finance, Economics and Politics more accessible. Amidst a pandemic, Warwick Congress adapted and overcame challenges and expected the unexpected. The in-person conference screamed variety, including professional footballers, authors and YouTubers with a unique humanitarian edge.

Notable speakers include: David Buttle, Andy Silvester, Adebayo Akinfenwa, Nitin Sharma and Pinsent Masons' representatives.

2020

In 2019, Warwick Congress bravely took on the growing and rapidly evolving Fourth Industrial Revolution, leading with the question 'where is humanity headed?' The 2-day conference focused on the economic, social and environmental consequences of the Fourth Industrial Revolution on businesses, governments and society as a whole.

Notable speakers include: Abhijit Akerkar, Marina Theodosiou, Brigette Hyacinth and Ian West.

2018

In 2018, Warwick Congress explored innovation and the social media revolution. Delving deep into the topic of Global Trade vs Embargoes, the conference addressed a new virtual era where goods and services are delivered to doorsteps everyday. Warwick Congress sought to provide a dynamic insight into whether the populist movements of the last few years are here to stay, or whether globalisation will pervade our future. With protectionist policy promised by the US administration and the uncertainties of Brexit looming over Europe, we asked: 'what is the fate of global free trade?'

Notable speakers include: Maria Carvalho, Michael Lewis, Dr. Katrin Tinn, Dr. Clair Gammage and Professor Gabriel Felbermayr.

2017

2017 marked the first annual conference of Warwick Congress, welcoming 500 delegates and 12 speakers to address 3 topics through different perspectives. Under the leadership of over 45 students, Warwick Congress aimed to explore issues in finance, law, politics and economics, to build an awareness of how these interconnected disciplines shape the discourse of our world. The themed categories of discussion were the environment, technology and Europe.

Notable speakers include: Abhinay Muthoo, Diana Wallis, Tony Cocker and Miriam Maes.
